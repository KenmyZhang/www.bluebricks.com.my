]]> A common question you’ll be asked when buying a house, renting an apartment or applying for a bank loan is: Can you provide proof of income?

Providing proof of income is expected for those wanting to secure a loan or sign a lease. Knowing what these documents are can be helpful if you were asked to provide one.

In this article, we explain what is proof of income and outline what documents can serve as proof of income in Malaysia.

What Is Proof of Income?

Let’s start with the basics. Proof of income is an official document that summarises your earnings over a specific period (usually a year). These documents prove that you are earning the amount you claim.

Generally, this documentation is required by landlords, banks, insurance providers, and credit card companies to determine your ability to make payments over a period of time.

Examples of situations where you would need your proof of income include:

  • Submitting tax returns
  • Purchasing health insurance
  • Obtaining a credit card or mortgage
  • Renting an apartment or a house
  • Getting a car loan, personal loan etc.

Banks & Proof of Income

When refinancing your loan, you are required to provide your income documents to your lender. These documents serve as proof of your personal and financial information.

The bank or lender you are applying for your loan from will review them to verify if you are earning enough income to settle your loan.

However, do take note that while each lender has its own unique application and eligibility requirements, many of them will ask for similar proof of income.

Therefore, by familiarising yourself with the types of income documents you may need to submit, you can prepare yourself for the application process and increase your chances of approval.

Proof of Income Required For a Bank Loan in Malaysia

There are many different types of employment in Malaysia. From full-time and part-time contracts to casual (freelance) jobs, fixed term, internships and more.

Moreover, each type of employment has its own wages and income documents. Therefore, it is essential to know the correct type of income documents you should submit when applying for any type of loan.

1. For Employees with a Stable Income

  • Photocopy of IC
  • Latest salary slips and bank statements of at least 3 months
  • Latest EPF Statements
  • Latest EA Form
  • Latest Income Tax Statement & Receipt
  • Employee Letter

2. For Sole Proprietors

  • Photocopy of IC
  • Business SSM certificate
  • Latest 6 months’ bank statement for Company’s Current Account
  • Latest Income Tax Statement & Receipt

3. For Private Limited Companies (Sdn Bhd)

  • Photocopy of IC
  • Business SSM certificate
  • Company Memorandum and Articles Association
  • Latest 2 years’ Financier Reports/Audit Reports
  • Latest 6 months’ bank statement for Company’s Current Account
  • Latest Income Tax Statement & Receipt

It is important to note that contract employees, such as freelancers, gig workers and part-timers, can have their loan applications rejected. This is because banks and financial institutions may assume that they are unable to repay their loan.

However, this issue can be resolved by providing the necessary financial documents, such as income tax documents or other proof of income. If you need any help preparing your income documents, our consultants at Bluebricks can help.

Challenges in Verifying Proof of Income

While the income verification process may seem straightforward, some challenges may arise during the application process. Three common income verification challenges include:

1. Providing Inaccurate Income Information

When applying for a housing loan, credit card, or property lease, applicants may accidentally include the wrong income, prolonging the lending process. Therefore applicants must always carefully review the information they have provided.

Moreover, there are some instances where an applicant intentionally makes their earnings appear higher than they are. This is because they want to make it seem as if they have the finances to manage ongoing payments.

However, applicants are advised never to put false information in their application documents, as this is against the law.

2. Submitting the Wrong Income Verification Documents

As there is a variety of proof of income documents available, it’s easy for many loan applicants to submit the wrong income document when applying for a service, a loan, or a rental.

While providing the wrong documents can be an accidental oversight, it can slow down the lending process.

The applicant will have to take more time to prepare their new income documents, and banks and other lenders will have to pay additional attention when conducting verifications.

3. You Do Not Meet The Required Income Threshold

Sometimes, you will not meet the income threshold when applying for a loan or a rental property. If this happens, you’ll likely need a co-signer on your application, or you can reapply once your financial situation improves.

If you do not have the time or are unsure if the documents you have prepared are correct, we recommend consulting an expert who can help you review your income documents.

Proof of Income – FAQs

The most important part of the income verification process is securing the appropriate income documents for a successful loan application.

You should ensure that you’re filling out forms completely and honestly, as there are penalties for intentionally adding incorrect information.

Are you interested in learning more about proof of income in Malaysia? We have compiled three of the top questions and answers that you may find helpful to broaden your knowledge about income documents.

Do all loans require proof of income?

Yes. Most lenders request proof of income before granting loan applications, especially for a car or home loan.
Some lenders or bank institutions may be willing to call your employer to verify your income, but in most cases, they prefer having hard copies of your proof of income.

Can I submit my bank statement as proof of income?

Yes, you can. Payslips, bank and EPF statements can be submitted as your proof of income and you must have more than RM3,000 as your income.
On the other hand, business owners must submit their bank statements, Suruhanjaya Syarikat Malaysia (SSM) statement and have a positive cash flow of more than RM50,000 for their loan application to be approved.

How can freelancers provide proof of income in Malaysia?

Malaysian freelancers must prepare their financial documents to show their creditworthiness, such as their latest payslips.
It is important to know that some banks prefer to offer loans to fixed-income earners only, while others are more flexible with variable-income earners (e.g., freelancers and commission-based jobs).

A strata title is a property title often issued for high-rise properties such as condominiums and apartments.

Once the leasehold period expires, the land returns to the State Authority. If the owner wants to retain the land, they will have to apply for a leasehold extension before the lease expires.

Unlike properties with a freehold title, leasehold title owners must abide by stricter rules and regulations.
